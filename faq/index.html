Beginner questions

Can I start Reformer Pilates with no experience?

Yes. Choose beginner-friendly classes and tell us your experience level in the match form.

How many classes per week should beginners do?

Most beginners progress well with 2 sessions/week for the first month.

What should I ask before booking my first studio?

Ask about onboarding process, class size, instructor experience, and language options.

Can I combine mat Pilates and Reformer?

Yes. A common approach is 2 Reformer classes plus 1 mat session per week.

Where can I get beginner tutorials first?

See beginner video guide and starter checklist.

Is Reformer Pilates safe if I have back pain?

It can be, especially with a teacher who can scale range and load. See this evidence-based back-pain guide and include your notes in the match form.

What should I wear to a Reformer class?

Wear comfortable, fitted clothing and grip socks if the studio requires them. Ask the studio about their policy before you go.

Reformer vs mat Pilates vs yoga

What’s better: Reformer Pilates or mat Pilates?

Reformer often helps with guided strength progression and feedback; mat Pilates is accessible and lower-cost. Many people combine both. See Reformer vs mat Pilates.

Reformer Pilates vs yoga on the mat: what’s the difference?

Reformer emphasizes resistance and controlled strength; yoga emphasizes mobility, breath, and flexibility. Both can improve function. See Reformer vs yoga on the mat.

Can I do Pilates if I’m not flexible?

Yes. Pilates can improve mobility over time. Start with beginner classes and progress gradually.

Is Reformer Pilates good for weight loss?

It can support body composition when paired with consistent training and nutrition. If weight loss is your goal, tell us so we can prioritize higher-frequency schedules and coaching.

Equipment + logistics

How many people are usually in a Reformer class?

It varies by studio and schedule. Smaller classes allow more feedback. If you prefer low ratios, request “small group” or “private”.

What’s the difference between Reformer, Tower, and Cadillac?

They’re different pieces of Pilates equipment with different setups and exercise options. If you’re new, start with a beginner onboarding class and ask the teacher what’s best for you.

Do I need grip socks?

Many studios recommend or require them for hygiene and safety. Ask the provider before you attend.

Can I request “Reformer only” sessions?

Yes. Mention equipment preference in your request and we shortlist suitable studios/teachers.
